### Invoicing & Billing
**The problem:** Someone still creates invoices by hand and chases payments one by one. Receipts pile up until it's time to reconcile them at month-end.

**The fix:** An invoice generates automatically the moment an order or project closes. Reminders go out on schedule, and everything syncs straight to your accounting tool.

**The result:** You get paid faster, with fewer mistakes and no chasing.

---

### Data Entry & Reporting
**The problem:** The same numbers get typed into three different systems, and reports get built by hand every week.

**The fix:** Data syncs automatically between the platforms you use. Reports generate on a schedule instead of a to-do list.

**The result:** That's hours back every week, and an end to copy-paste mistakes.

---

### Customer Onboarding & Follow-ups
**The problem:** New customers fall through the cracks. Follow-ups get forgotten when things get busy.

**The fix:** A welcome sequence goes out automatically over WhatsApp, Telegram, or email, and your team gets a nudge the moment a follow-up is due.

**The result:** Customers stop waiting on someone to remember them.

---

### Order & Inventory Tracking
**The problem:** Stock counts live in a spreadsheet that's always a little out of date.

**The fix:** Inventory updates automatically across channels, with alerts when stock runs low.

**The result:** Stockouts drop, and reconciliation stops eating an afternoon each month.

---

### Internal Approvals & Admin Requests
**The problem:** Approval requests get buried in email or chat, and it's unclear who's supposed to sign off.

**The fix:** Requests move through a structured approval flow in Slack, Discord, or WhatsApp, with automatic routing and reminders built in.

**The result:** Decisions move faster, and there's a clear record of who approved what.

# PAGE: How It Works

## Header
**Kicker:** Less admin. More time.

### Headline
**From messy process to automated flow, step by step.**

### Intro
Automation only works if it's built around how your business actually runs, not a generic template. Here's the process, from first call to go-live.

---

### Step 1: Discovery Call
A short, no-commitment call (about 30 minutes) where we ask what's eating your team's time. You don't need to prepare anything. Just show up and describe how things currently work, warts and all.

### Step 2: Process Mapping
We sit down with you or your team to map the process in detail, including the workarounds nobody's written down. This is usually where we find time savings you didn't know were there.

### Step 3: Build & Test
We build the automation on the tools you already use and test it against real data before it ever touches your live operations. You're not the QA team; we are.

### Step 4: Go Live
The workflow goes live with a monitoring period built in. We watch closely for the first stretch, catching anything a test environment couldn't predict.

### Step 5: Ongoing Support
Once it's running, it runs quietly in the background. We check in periodically, and you can reach us any time something changes on your end, whether that's a new tool or a new process.

---

### What we need from you
- One point of contact who knows the process
- Access to the tools involved (we'll tell you exactly what, nothing more)
- A short review window during testing, so we can confirm it matches how you actually work
